# SNUPI research slides interview checklist (EN)

Use this only for information that is still missing after reading the user's Markdown brief, notes, paper URLs, or PDFs.

## Core deck questions
1. What is the presentation type: literature review, single-paper deep-dive, seminar update, or research meeting?
2. Who is the audience, and how technical should the deck be?
3. What is the one-sentence goal of this presentation?
4. What material already exists: Markdown notes, paper URLs, PDFs, experiment results, or meeting notes?
5. What deadline or length constraint matters most?

## Research questions
1. Which papers or sources are mandatory?
2. Which figure, table, or equation must appear if possible?
3. What result or claim should the audience remember?
4. What critiques, limitations, or open questions should be included?
5. Are there lab-specific takeaways, follow-up ideas, or action items?

## Slide-writing reminders
- Prefer a short narrative over a complete paper transcription.
- Build each slide around one idea and one dominant visual anchor.
- Ask follow-up questions only when the answer changes the outline or slide content.
- If the user already gave structured notes, confirm missing gaps instead of re-interviewing from scratch.
